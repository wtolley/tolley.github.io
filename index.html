William J. Tolley

I'm currently a Visiting Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Washington and Lee University and in the final stages of my PhD at Arizona State University. My work is rooted in systems and network security, where I design and analyze privacy-focused technologies and countermeasures against censorship and surveillance. I believe that teaching is more than just passing on knowledge—it’s about immersing students in the kind of problems that challenge them to think deeply, adapt, and create. My approach integrates my research and practical experiences, bridging the gap between theory and impactful application.

I am seeking a tenure-track position at a liberal arts college starting in Fall 2025. My goal is to cultivate an engaging, collaborative space where students become active thinkers and problem-solvers, equipped not just with technical skills but with the mindset to apply them meaningfully in a complex world.
